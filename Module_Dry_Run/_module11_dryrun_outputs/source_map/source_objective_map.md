- Handouts: Handout 11.1a Nutrition Crossword Puzzle, Handout 11.1b Nutrition Crossword Puzzle KEY (internal/instructor key)

- Source pages: [4, 5]
- Weighted theory minutes: 8
- Content outline: food and fluids provide energy; promote growth and tissue repair; regulate body functions; meet basic survival need; nutrition is food and relationship to health; balanced diet has sufficient nutrients and about 1500 mL/day adult minimum fluids

- Evaluation: Written test
- Handouts: None mapped
- Manual skills: None mapped
- Required activities: Food and Fluid Needs Sort, Balanced Diet Builder, Hydration Minimum Check
- Boundary: report changes/risks to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.

- Teaching strategies: Lecture/Discussion; Handout 11.3a Food Content Exercise; Handout 11.3b Food Content Exercise KEY; Food label/container review

- Handouts: Handout 11.3a Food Content Exercise, Handout 11.3b Food Content Exercise KEY (internal/instructor key)

- Source pages: [7, 8]
- Weighted theory minutes: 12

- Teaching strategies: Lecture/Discussion; Discuss common food allergies
- Evaluation: Written test; Identify age-related nutrition needs and report to licensed nurse; Report allergies to licensed nurse
- Handouts: None mapped
- Manual skills: None mapped

- Boundary: report changes/risks to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.

### Objective 7 — Describe therapeutic diets commonly ordered for patients/residents and the responsibilities of the Nurse Assistant
- Source pages: [8, 9]
- Weighted theory minutes: 15
- Content outline: therapeutic diets: low sodium, diabetic, low fat, low cholesterol, liquid, bland; liquid diets: clear/full/thickened nectar or honey; special consistency: mechanical soft, pureed, soft; NA responsibilities: ordered diet, ID/diet card checks, report problems, open containers, I&O monitoring, food intake recording, refusal handling, no extra food/condiments without checking, temperatures, do not mix pureed foods, upright eating
- Teaching strategies: Lecture/Discussion; Jeopardy game from prior objectives; Review allowed/restricted foods/fluids for therapeutic diets; Discuss nutritional adequacy; Conference with Speech/Occupational Therapy
- Evaluation: Written test; Correctly identify residents and check diet card for name/order/instructions/allergies; Accurately record intake; Report dietary habit changes
- Handouts: None mapped
- Manual skills: None mapped
- Required activities: Therapeutic Diet Match, Diet Card Safety Gate, I&O and Intake Record Practice
- Boundary: report changes/risks to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.

### Objective 8 — Describe proper techniques for feeding patients/residents
- Source pages: [9, 10, 11]
- Weighted theory minutes: 18
- Content outline: comfortable feeding atmosphere and eye-level seating; encourage participation and pace; do not rush; adaptive equipment and room/dining service; alternate liquids/solids; check swallowing; high-risk dysphagia/choking groups; follow feeding plans, small pieces/amounts, wait to swallow; signs of dysphagia and reporting; upright during/30 minutes after eating, thicken liquids as ordered, choking signs
- Teaching strategies: Lecture/Discussion; Sensory impairment feeding simulation; Discuss feeding/being fed experience; Pudding/Jello/yogurt containers; Liquids with/without straws; Manual Skills 11.8a-c; Review Manual Skill 12.4 choking victim
- Evaluation: Written test; Identify high-risk symbols; Use proper feeding techniques with/without swallowing difficulty
- Handouts: None mapped
- Manual skills: Manual Skill 11.8a Preparing the Patient/resident for Mealtime, Manual Skill 11.8b Feeding a Patient/resident Who Cannot Feed Self, Manual Skill 11.8c Assisting the Patient/resident Who Can Feed Self, Review Manual Skill 12.4 Choking Victim - Conscious and Unconscious
- Required activities: Mealtime Setup Coach, Dysphagia Sign Alert, Safe Feeding Pace Simulation
- Boundary: report changes/risks to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.

- Handouts: None mapped

- Boundary: report changes/risks to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.
