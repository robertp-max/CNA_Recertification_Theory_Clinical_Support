## Preserved Research Baseline

- BRN approves continuing education providers, not individual courses.
- Each CEP must offer course content and use instructors that meet CCR section 1456 and 1457 requirements.
- The BRN application packet requires the application fee, completed provider application, Course Information form, Instructor Information form, proposed flyer or brochure, and sample certificate of completion.
- The application fee is listed as $750 in the planning baseline and is earned and non-refundable.
- The application requires FEIN for a business/corporation or SSN/ITIN for an individual applicant; missing FEIN/SSN delays processing.
- Complete application processing is generally 8 to 12 weeks.
- The provider number expires two years from initial issuance and is not transferable if the business is sold.
- BRN must be notified within 30 days of mailing address, organizational structure, or course coordinator/person responsible changes.
- Courses cannot be offered under an expired CEP number.
- Course content must be relevant to RN practice and relate to scientific/technical nursing knowledge or direct/indirect patient/client care.
- Learning experiences should enhance RN knowledge above the licensure level.
- Course offerings must be at least one hour; each contact hour is at least 50 minutes of instruction in an organized learning environment.
- Partial credit for part of a course may not be granted unless structured as separately completed segments.
- Behavioral objectives are required; instructor goals are not acceptable as learner objectives.
- Course evaluations should address whether objectives were met, applicability/usability, instructor mastery, teaching methods, mechanics, and comments.
- RN instructors must have a current valid RN license, be free from BRN discipline, and be qualified by degree/experience, recent teaching, or recent specialized experience.
- Non-RN instructors must have appropriate license/certification when applicable, specialized training, and at least one year of recent experience in the subject taught.
- Proof of attendance/certificate must include all required information and must be issued within 90 days after course conclusion.
- Blank certificates are prohibited.
- The certificate must tell the RN to retain proof of attendance for 4 years.
- Advertising must include the provider statement after approval: “Provider approved by the California Board of Registered Nursing, Provider Number _____, for _____ contact hours.”
- Advertising must include refund/nonattendance policy, provider’s official name, and clear description/objectives.
- Avoid the term “CEUs” because BRN says CEUs are given by colleges/universities only; use “contact hours.”
- Provider records must be kept for 4 years in one California location or BRN-approved location.
- Records must include course outline, date(s) and place(s) given, CV/resumes for instructors, name and license number or other identification of RNs taking the course, and record of certificate issued.
- Provider must have written policies for refund/nonattendance, cancellation notification, and refund timing.
- BRN can withdraw approval or deny application for material misrepresentation or failure to comply with governing law/regulations.
