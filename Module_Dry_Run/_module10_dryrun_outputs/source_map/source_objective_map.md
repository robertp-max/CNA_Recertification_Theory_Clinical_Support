### Objective 3 — Discuss the use of temperature as an indicator of body function
- Source pages: [5, 6, 7]
- Weighted theory minutes: 22
- Content outline: temperature balances heat gained/lost; hypothalamus regulates temperature; heat production via cellular activity, metabolism, muscle activity, hormones; heat loss via skin, lungs, elimination; heat conservation by reduced perspiration/blood flow and shivering; oral, rectal, tympanic, axillary, temporal sites; glass/digital/electronic/disposable/tympanic/temporal thermometers; normal ranges and route differences; contraindications and abnormal reporting
- Teaching strategies: Lecture/Discussion; Display thermometer types; Identify types; Demonstration/return demonstration with thermometers and contraindications; Manual Skills 10.3a-e; Ear temperature visual aid
- Evaluation: Written test; Demonstrate oral/axillary/temporal/rectal temperature; Use mercury-free glass/electronic/digital/tympanic/temporal thermometers; Report abnormal temperature
- Handouts: None mapped
- Manual skills: Manual Skills 10.3a Oral Temperature, Manual Skills 10.3b Axillary Temperature, Manual Skills 10.3c Tympanic Temperature, Manual Skills 10.3d Rectal Temperature, Manual Skills 10.3e Electronic Temperature
- Required activities: Heat Balance Simulator, Thermometer Museum, Temperature Route Choice
- Boundary: abnormal findings/complaints report to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.

### Objective 5 — Describe the circulatory system as it relates to pulse, and identify the pulse sites
- Source pages: [8]
- Weighted theory minutes: 8
- Content outline: pulse is arterial wall expansion after heart contraction; count palpated expansions for pulse rate; average adult pulse 60-100/minute; pulse sites: carotid, apical, brachial, radial, femoral, popliteal, dorsalis pedis
- Teaching strategies: Lecture/Discussion; Pulse-site visual aid; Role play; Advanced pulse deficit research/demonstration
- Evaluation: Written test; Locate major pulse sites
- Handouts: None mapped
- Manual skills: None mapped
- Required activities: Pulse Site Body Map, Heart-to-Artery Animation, Site Match Speed Round
- Boundary: abnormal findings/complaints report to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.

### Objective 6 — Describe factors that increase and decrease pulse, and the qualities to observe in taking a pulse
- Source pages: [8, 9]
- Weighted theory minutes: 12
- Content outline: increase pulse: exercise, fever, hemorrhage, pain, shock, strong emotions, tachycardia over 100; decrease pulse: sleep/rest, depression, drugs, athlete conditioning, bradycardia below 60; qualities: rate, rhythm, strength/force, thready, bounding; report irregular rhythm even if rate normal
- Teaching strategies: Lecture/Discussion; Demonstration/return demonstration for radial/apical pulse full minute; Role play; Manual Skills 10.6a/10.6b
- Evaluation: Written test; Measure radial/brachial pulse for full minute; Report abnormal pulse
- Handouts: None mapped
- Manual skills: Manual Skills 10.6a Radial Pulse, Manual Skills 10.6b Apical Pulse, Manual Skill Count and Record Radial Pulse
- Required activities: Pulse Detective, Beat Trainer, One-Full-Minute Practice
- Boundary: abnormal findings/complaints report to licensed nurse; no online clinical competency claim.
