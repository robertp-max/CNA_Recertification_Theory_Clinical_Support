Module 12: Emergency Procedures
Minimum Number of Theory Hours: 2
Recommended Clinical Hours: 1

Statement of Purpose:
The purpose of this unit is to introduce the student to the concepts and procedures related to emergency procedures, signs and
symptoms of distress, and the role of the Nurse Assistant in Long Term Care (LTC) in the response to immediate and temporary
intervention in emergency situations.

         d. Enlargement of pupils

C. Cerebrovascular accident (CVA, Stroke, or brain attack)
    1. A disturbance or obstruction of the flow of blood to a particular
         area of the brain with subsequent death of tissue
    2. Signs and Symptoms
         a. Hemiplegia or weakness of one side of the body or
              numbness or tingling on one side of the body
         b. Aphasia-difficulty in speaking or understanding speech
         c. Headache
         d. Vision changes, blurred vision, pupils unequal
         e. Facial changes
              1) Cheeks may "puff "on exhalation
              2) One eyelid or eye may droop
              3) Face may appear asymmetrical
              4) Drooling
         f. Loss of bowel or bladder control
         g. Shaking or trembling
    3. Give Me 5 for Stroke Tool from American Stroke Association
         a. Walk (Is their balance off?)
         b. Talk (Is their speech slurred or is their face droopy?)
         c. Reach (Is one side weak or numb?)
         d. See (Is their vision all or partly lost)
         e. Feel (Is their headache severe?)
    4. FAST from American Stroke Association
         a. Face drooping
         b. Arm weakness
         c. Speech difficulty
         d. Time to call 9-1-1
    5. Nurse Assistant Role
         a. Call for help loudly and pull emergency call light if available
         b. Remain calm

D. Syncope, fainting episode
    1. A feeling of dizziness with possible temporary loss of
         consciousness
    2. Signs and Symptoms
         a. Dizziness
         b. Visual changes-temporary loss of vision
         c. Pallor or paleness of the skin
         d. Cool, moist skin
         e. Eyes may roll back
         f. Unsteadiness or loss of upright position (patient/resident
              may fall)
         g. Weak pulse
    3. Nursing Assistant Role
         a. Before loss of consciousness and during dizziness
              1) Remain calm, call for help loudly, pull call light
              2) Assist to floor, protect from injury
              3) If sitting, place head towards knees
              4) If lying flat on back, elevate legs slightly if no spinal,

                   head or back injuries (If unsure, leave flat on back)
              5) Loosen tight or binding clothing
              6) Observe for any changes in condition
         b. After loss of consciousness
              1) Raise legs approximately 8-12 inches if no spinal, head

                   or back injuries (If unsure, leave flat on back)

E. Seizures; convulsions or epilepsy

    1. An interference with the normal electrical activity of the brain
         with subsequent changes in mental status

    2. Types of seizures and related Signs and Symptoms
         a. Absence or partial (petit mal) seizure
              1) A mild blackout

              2) Looks as though daydreaming

         b. Generalized tonic-clonic or grand mal seizure
              1) Uncontrolled muscular contractions

              2) Can be minimal to major with possible violent head
                   jerking

              3) May be frothing at the mouth

              4) May be loss of bowel and bladder control
    3. Nurse Assistant Role

         a. Assist patient/resident to ground safely

         b. Note time

         c. Cushion head
         d. Remain calm, call for help loudly, and pull emergency call

              light
         e. Stay with patient/resident and observe

         f. If possible, gently turn head to one side to reduce risk of
              choking (This may not be possible in a violent seizure)

         g. Loosen clothing and/or jewelry
         h. Pad any items that may be dangerous to the

              patient/resident or move items away from
              patient/resident (i.e. furniture)
         i. Do NOT attempt to restrain nor put anything into
              patient's/resident's mouth
         j. Note time seizure ends
         k. Follow licensed nurse instructions to assist with putting
              patient/resident into recovery position

F. Insulin Shock
    1. Definition
         a. Hypoglycemia
         b. Condition resulting from an overdose of insulin resulting in
              reduction of the blood sugar level below normal
         c. May develop due to an insulin-dependent patient/resident
              skipping meals or snacks, stress, diarrhea and vomiting or
              possible medication reaction
    2. Signs and Symptoms
         a. Pale, moist skin
         b. Rapid bounding pulse
         c. Headache, confusion, weakness
         d. Anxiety, excitement
         e. Hunger
         f. Low blood pressure (hypotension)
         g. Unconsciousness
    3. Nurse Assistant Role
         a. Stay with patient/resident
         b. Remain calm, call for help loudly, and pull emergency call
              light
         c. Administer orange juice, milk, or snack if instructed by
              licensed nurse

G. Hemorrhaging, severe bleeding
    1. An extreme or unexpected loss of blood
    2. Signs and Symptoms
         a. External bleeding
              1) Bleeding in spurts (arterial)
              2) Steady flow of blood (venous)
              3) Slow oozing of blood (capillary)
         b. Internal bleeding
              1) Coughing up bright red blood
              2) Vomit that has the appearance of coffee grounds
              3) Blood in urine or stool (stool may be black and tarry in
                   appearance)

    3. Nurse Assistant Role
         a. Remain calm, call for assistance loudly, and pull
              emergency call light
         b. Stay with patient/resident
         c. Observe standard precautions, wear gloves
         d. Apply direct pressure, with gauze pad, over area that is
              bleeding
         e. Elevate affected limb
         f. Do not offer food or drink
         g. Keep patient/resident calm and cover to keep warm

H. Shock
    1. Failure of the cardiovascular system to provide sufficient
         blood circulation to every part of the body
    2. Signs and Symptoms
         a. Skin pale, cold and clammy, or moist
         b. Pulse rapid (over 100) and weak; low or falling blood
              pressure
         c. Respiration shallow, irregular, or labored
         d. Eyes dull and lackluster

         e. Nausea, vomiting, and/or thirst
         f. Confusion, anxiety, restlessness

         g. May collapse (faint)
    3. Nurse Assistant Role

         a. Remain calm, call for help loudly, and pull emergency call
              light

         b. Stay with patient/resident, give reassurance
         c. Maintain an open airway (head tilt-chin lift or modified chin

              lift)
         d. Do not give food or drink
         e. Cover patient/resident to keep warm

    1. Increase or decrease in the effort and frequency of breathing
         movements

    3. Perform CPR only if trained
         a. Check to see if patient/resident is conscious
         b. Circulation
              1) Check for circulation by feeling for a pulse, palpating
                   the carotid artery
              2) If no definite pulse within 10 seconds, give chest
                   compressions
         c. Airway
              1) Open the airway if patient/resident unconscious
              2) Use head tilt, chin lift or modified chin lift
         d. Breathing
              1) Check for breathing by looking, listening and feeling
              2) Give two full breaths using a barrier device (pocket
                   mask)
              3) A mask must be used by the Nurse Assistant to do
                   rescue breathing
         e. Circulation
              1) Check for circulation by feeling for a pulse, palpating
                   the carotid artery
              2) If no definite pulse within ten seconds, give chest
                   compressions
         f. Sequence; continue per current standards with 30
              compressions to 2 ventilations (30:2)

C. General guidelines for a emergency situation with a patient/resident
    1. Stay calm
    2. Call for help � gain assistance of licensed nurse
    3. Charge nurse will initiate EMS system by calling 911
    4. Remain with patient/resident
    5. Intervene at level of competence as directed by licensed nurse
    6. Reassure/calm patient/resident
    7. Emergency/crash cart; knowing the location of the crash cart is
         mandatory at most facilities
    8. Automated External Defibrillator (AED)

         a. Be aware of AED location

         b. Chance of survival is greater with early defibrillation
         c. Must be trained to use defibrillators

MANUAL SKILL: Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver) for the Conscious and Unconscious Patient/resident

CONSCIOUS PATIENT/RESIDENT

STEPS:

1. Remain calm, call RN STAT, remain with patient/resident, and ask patient/resident if he/she is choking.
2. Identify knowledge of first aid for choking.
3. If the patient/resident can cough, continue to observe.
4. If the patient/resident is sitting or standing, stand behind him/her.
5. When the patient/resident cannot speak, cough, or breathe, apply abdominal thrusts:

    a. Wrap your arms around the patient's/resident's waist.
    b. Make a fist with one hand.
    c. Place the thumb side of your fist against the patient's/resident's abdomen � just below the lower end of the sternum and

         above the navel.
    d. Grasp the fist with the opposite hand.
    e. Using both hands, push forcefully with the thumb side of fist against the midline of the patient's/resident's abdomen,

         inward and upward with a quick trust.
    f. Repeat until the foreign body comes out or the patient/resident loses consciousness.
    g. When normal breathing returns, watch for several minutes to be sure that breathing continues.

UNCONSCIOUS PATIENT/RESIDENT

EQUIPMENT:

Gloves (if available)
Ventilation device (i.e. ambu bag)
Pocket mask (if available)

STEPS:

1. Call for help, but do not leave patient/resident.
2. Lower patient/resident to floor and position on back.
3. Apply gloves (if available), tilt head, open airway by pushing on forehead and lifting chin with fingertips.
4. Try two slow breaths using pocket mask, or ambu bag (one breath every five seconds).
5. Straddle patient's/resident's thighs.
6. Place heel of one hand on patient's/resident's abdomen below the lower end of the sternum and above the navel, place second

    hand over first (fingers pointing toward head), give six to ten thrusts.
7. Remove object if seen.
8. Attempt to give breaths through pocket mask or ambu bag.
9. If still obstructed repeat thrusts, checking for object and giving breaths until object is dislodged.
10. Continue until airway is open, help arrives, or rescuer cannot continue.
