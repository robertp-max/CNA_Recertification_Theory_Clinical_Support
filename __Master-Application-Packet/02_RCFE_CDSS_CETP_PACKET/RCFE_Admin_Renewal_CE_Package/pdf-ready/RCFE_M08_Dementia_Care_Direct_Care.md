## Module description

Live/instructor-interactive dementia module focused on DIRECT CARE: Alzheimer's disease and related dementias, stages and progression, person-centered communication, supporting activities of daily living, responding to unmet needs, and reducing distress through relationship-based direct care. Delivered live so administrators practice and discuss techniques with peers and the instructor. Counts toward the required 8 dementia hours.

## Measurable learning objectives

- Describe the common types and stages of dementia and their care implications.
- Apply person-centered communication techniques to a resident with dementia in a live practice.
- Adapt ADL support to preserve dignity and remaining abilities.
- Interpret behavior as communication of unmet needs and select a direct-care response.
- Coach direct-care staff on a relationship-based dementia care approach.

## Required readings / learning content sections

- **M08-L1 - Dementia Types, Stages, and Progression (Facilitated)** (60 min): Alzheimer's vs. vascular/Lewy/frontotemporal, trajectory, cognition/function changes, care planning impact.
- **M08-L2 - Person-Centered Communication Practice** (60 min): Approach, validation, redirection, sensory and environmental cues, avoiding argument/correction.
- **M08-L3 - ADL Support and Dignity** (60 min): Dressing/bathing/eating support, choice and cueing, reducing resistance through approach.
- **M08-L4 - Behavior as Communication: Direct-Care Response** (60 min): Identifying triggers/unmet needs, non-pharmacological responses, staff coaching script.
