## Module description

Self-paced review of the statutory and regulatory framework governing California Residential Care Facilities for the Elderly (Health & Safety Code Division 2, Chapter 3.2 and Title 22, Division 6, Chapter 8), licensing authority, citation and appeal processes, mandated postings, and the administrator's duty to maintain current policies and procedures. Renewal-level refresher; does not grant initial certification.

## Measurable learning objectives

- Identify the licensing authority and the primary statutes and regulations that govern RCFE operation.
- Differentiate between Type A, Type B, and Type C deficiencies and the associated civil penalty and appeal timelines.
- Locate at least five mandated facility postings and the legal basis for each.
- Given a regulatory scenario, determine the administrator's required reporting or corrective action and timeline.
- Update a facility policy statement so that it is consistent with current RCFE regulatory requirements.

## Required readings / learning content sections

- **M01-L1 - Regulatory Framework and Licensing Authority** (55 min): HSC Division 2 Ch 3.2, Title 22 Div 6 Ch 8, CDSS/Community Care Licensing role, scope vs. ARF/ADHC.
- **M01-L2 - Inspections, Citations, Civil Penalties, and Appeals** (55 min): Type A/B deficiencies, immediate vs. non-immediate jeopardy, plan of correction, appeal rights and timelines.
- **M01-L3 - Mandated Postings, Disclosures, and Recordkeeping Duties** (55 min): License posting, complaint poster/ombudsman number, emergency disclosure plan, resident records retention duties.
- **M01-L4 - Applied Compliance: Policy Updates and Administrator Action** (75 min): Fictional facility receives a deficiency notice; learner drafts a corrected policy and plan-of-correction outline.
