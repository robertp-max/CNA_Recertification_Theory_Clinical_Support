## U01 - U01 - Vital Signs I: Purpose, Terminology, Temperature, Pain Observation (10 questions)

Moodle import category: `CNA/U01`

### CNA-U01-Q01 (mcq, easy)

**Objective:** Explain the purpose of vital sign measurement in resident monitoring.  
**Source:** CCCCO NATP Module 10 - Vital Signs  
**SME status:** SME review recommended

What is the primary reason a nurse assistant measures and records a resident's vital signs?

- A. To diagnose new medical conditions independently
- B. To provide objective baseline and change data the licensed nurse uses to monitor the resident
- C. To decide which medications the resident should receive
- D. To replace the need for nurse assessment

**Correct answer:** B  
**Rationale:** Nurse assistants collect and report objective data; diagnosis and medication decisions are outside the CNA scope.

### CNA-U01-Q02 (mcq, easy)

**Objective:** Identify the standard vital signs.  
**Source:** CCCCO NATP Module 10 - Vital Signs  
**SME status:** SME review recommended

Which set best represents the four standard vital signs?

- A. Temperature, pulse, respirations, blood pressure
- B. Height, weight, intake, output
- C. Pain, mood, appetite, sleep
- D. Pulse, oxygen tank level, room temperature, weight

**Correct answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The four standard vital signs are temperature, pulse, respirations, and blood pressure; pain is often documented as a fifth measure.

### CNA-U02-Q01 (mcq, easy)

**Objective:** Demonstrate accurate pulse measurement technique.  
**Source:** CCCCO NATP Module 10 - Vital Signs  
**SME status:** SME review recommended

When counting a resting radial pulse, which practice best supports an accurate count?

- A. Count for 5 seconds and multiply by 12
- B. Count for a full 30-60 seconds, noting rate, rhythm, and strength
- C. Count only if the resident reports feeling unwell
- D. Estimate based on how the resident looks

**Correct answer:** B  
**Rationale:** Counting for a longer interval and noting rhythm and strength improves accuracy, especially with irregular pulses.

### CNA-U02-Q02 (mcq, medium)

**Objective:** Demonstrate accurate respiration counting.  
**Source:** CCCCO NATP Module 10 - Vital Signs  
**SME status:** SME review recommended

To count respirations accurately, the nurse assistant should:

- A. Tell the resident you are counting their breathing
- B. Count discreetly, often right after the pulse, so the resident does not consciously change breathing
- C. Ask the resident to breathe deeply while counting
- D. Count only the inhalations during exercise

**Correct answer:** B  
**Rationale:** Counting discreetly avoids voluntary changes in breathing; one rise and fall equals one respiration.
