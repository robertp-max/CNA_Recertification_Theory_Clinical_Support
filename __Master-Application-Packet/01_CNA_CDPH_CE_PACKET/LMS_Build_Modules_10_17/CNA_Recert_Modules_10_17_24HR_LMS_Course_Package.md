This package converts the source **CCCCO Nurse Assistant Model Curriculum (Revised December 2018), Modules 10-17** into an LMS-ready, **24-hour online continuing education (CE) course delivered as 12 units x 2 online CE hours** for the California CNA two-year renewal cycle, built for Moodle.

- The 24 online CE hours are organized as **12 units x 2 hours**, split into **Year 1 (U01-U06, 12 hours)** and **Year 2 (U07-U12, 12 hours)**.
- Source Modules 10-17 are preserved as the content backbone and mapped under each unit (Section C/F). No new module topics are invented; no standalone Infection Control unit is added (not source-backed within Modules 10-17).
- Online CE hours are kept strictly separate from clinical hours. This package does **not** claim hands-on clinical competency validation and does **not** claim clinical hours.
- All learner-facing artifacts carry draft/pending-approval language. No real approval, provider, or course numbers are used.
- Every scenario is fictional and de-identified. No PHI, no real resident/facility names, no real clinical records.

This 12-unit x 2-hour structure is aligned across the application packet and the LMS build; there is no unit-vs-module structure conflict to resolve.

## F. Unit Blueprints (U01-U12)

Each unit = 2 online CE hours. Objectives are the source CCCCO performance standards, restated as measurable online-CE objectives (cognitive/observation/reporting level - no hands-on competency claim). Each unit's LMS activity set: Page (objectives + banner) -> Lesson -> interactive practice -> fictional scenario -> knowledge check -> documentation/reflection.

### U01 - Vital Signs I (Year 1 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 10
Objectives: define vital-signs terminology; state adult temperature normal ranges and routes; identify factors that raise/lower temperature; observe pain objectively and report within scope.

### U02 - Vital Signs II (Year 1 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 10
Objectives: measure/observe pulse, respirations, and blood pressure concepts and adult normal ranges; distinguish normal vs reportable findings; record T-P-R and BP accurately within CNA documentation scope; report abnormal findings to the licensed nurse.

### U03 - Nutrition, Hydration, Diets, Feeding Assistance, and Aspiration Awareness (Year 1 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 11
Objectives: define nutrition/hydration terminology; match common therapeutic diets to the CNA role; apply safe feeding-assistance and aspiration-prevention precautions; respect cultural/religious dietary needs; observe-and-report alternative nutrition routes.

### U04 - Emergency Procedures, Distress Recognition, Choking Response, and Facility Codes (Year 1 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 12
Objectives: recognize signs of resident distress; sequence scope-safe immediate actions (stay, call the nurse, activate the EMS chain); describe within-scope response to a conscious choking adult; match facility emergency codes. Online CE only - no hands-on CPR/first-aid certification is claimed.

### U05 - Long Term Care Patient/Resident I: Aging, Body Systems, and Common Conditions (Year 1 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 13
Objectives: summarize aging changes by body system; describe common conditions and complications of immobility the CNA observes and reports; relate basic anatomy/physiology to scope-safe observation.

### U06 - Long Term Care Patient/Resident II: Dementia-Sensitive Care, Psychosocial Needs, and Community Resources (Year 1 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 13
Objectives: apply dementia-sensitive, person-centered approaches and scope-safe de-escalation; describe psychosocial/spiritual needs and supports; identify community resources for elderly residents.

### U07 - Rehabilitative Nursing and Restorative Care (Year 2 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 14
Objectives: explain restorative-care philosophy; describe the CNA role on the rehab team; promote ADLs and self-care; identify adaptive/comfort devices; describe ROM types and immobility-complication prevention at the assist/observe/report level.

### U08 - Observation and Charting I: Objective/Subjective Observation, Terminology, and Reporting Cues (Year 2 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 15
Objectives: distinguish objective vs subjective observation and the senses used; identify common medical word elements/abbreviations; recognize reporting cues and escalate within scope.

### U09 - Observation and Charting II: Charting Documents, Legal Documentation, and Change Reporting (Year 2 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 15
Objectives: identify charting document types; apply accurate, timely, factual, scope-safe recording and correction practices; complete an objective incident report; report changes in condition. CNAs document observations, not diagnoses.

### U10 - Death and Dying: Grief, Comfort, Hospice Awareness, and Postmortem Care Boundaries (Year 2 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 16
Objectives: describe the five stages of grief; apply scope-safe approaches to emotional/spiritual needs; list rights of the dying resident; differentiate signs of approaching vs biological death; describe comfort, hospice-team support, and postmortem-care boundaries within scope.

### U11 - Patient/Resident Abuse I: Rights, Abuse Types, Prevention, and Risk Recognition (Year 2 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 17
Objectives: define abuse terminology and types; explain resident rights; describe the CNA role in prevention and risk recognition.

### U12 - Patient/Resident Abuse II: Mandated Reporting, Confidentiality, Documentation, and Final Applied Scenario (Year 2 - 2 online CE hours) - Source: Module 17
Objectives: describe the mandated-reporter duty, reporting pathways, and timelines; maintain confidentiality; document objectively; apply learning in a final applied scenario.
