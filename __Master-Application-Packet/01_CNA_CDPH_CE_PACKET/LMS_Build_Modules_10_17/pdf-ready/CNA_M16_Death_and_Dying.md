## Module Description
Introduces death as a normal stage of life, the stages of grief, and the physical/psychological/spiritual needs of dying residents and their families, with the CNA's scope-safe role in comfort, dignity, hospice support, and postmortem care.

## Measurable Learning Objectives
The learner will be able to:
1. Define key end-of-life terminology (palliative care, hospice, DNR, advance directive, postmortem care, rigor mortis, mottling, cachexia).
2. Describe the five stages of the grieving process per Dr. Kübler-Ross.
3. Describe scope-safe approaches to the emotional and spiritual needs of dying residents and families.
4. List the rights of the dying resident.
5. Differentiate common signs of approaching death from biological death.
6. Identify comfort/dignity measures and the CNA's role with a licensed hospice nurse; describe CNA responsibilities in postmortem care.

## Required Learning Content Sections
- Death as a normal life stage; the five stages of grief.
- Emotional/spiritual needs; rights of the dying resident.
- Signs of approaching death vs biological death.
- Comfort/dignity measures; hospice philosophy and CNA role.
- Postmortem care responsibilities.

## Fictional / De-identified Resident Scenario (S-M16)
A fictional family member is visibly angry at staff while their loved one (*Resident F, fictional*) is dying. **Decision points:** (1) What compassionate, scope-safe response does the CNA choose? (2) What comfort/dignity measures apply? (3) What does the CNA report and to whom? **Scope guardrail:** provide comfort and presence within scope, listen, escalate concerns to the nurse — the CNA does not pronounce death or make clinical/prognostic statements.

## Knowledge Check Blueprint
8–10 items: grief stages, dying rights, signs of approaching/biological death, hospice role, postmortem care.

## Assessment Items (seed)
1. (Matching) Match each Kübler-Ross stage (denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance) to its description.
2. (MCQ) A dying resident's family member is angry at staff. Best CNA response? A) Argue back B) Respond with compassion, listen, and report concerns to the nurse C) Ignore them D) Leave the unit
3. (TF) A CNA may pronounce a resident dead.
4. (MCQ) Postmortem care by the CNA follows: A) The CNA's own judgment B) Facility policy and within CNA scope C) The family's medical orders D) No procedure

## Internal Answer Key Appendix (admin/faculty only — do NOT publish)
