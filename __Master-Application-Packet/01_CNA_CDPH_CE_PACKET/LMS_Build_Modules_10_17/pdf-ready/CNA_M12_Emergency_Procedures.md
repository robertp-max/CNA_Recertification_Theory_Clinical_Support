## Module Description
Introduces the concepts and procedures related to emergencies, signs and symptoms of distress, and the CNA's role in long-term care for immediate and temporary intervention. **Critical:** this is online CE knowledge only — it does **not** certify or validate hands-on CPR, AED, or Heimlich/abdominal-thrust skills; hands-on certification is a separate program.

## Measurable Learning Objectives
The learner will be able to:
1. Define key emergency terminology (syncope, hypoxia, cyanosis, cardiac arrest, respiratory distress, recovery position, EMS, DNR, AED).
2. Recognize common signs/symptoms of resident distress and describe the CNA's prevention/response role.
3. Sequence the CNA's immediate, scope-safe actions in a medical emergency (stay with resident, call for help/the nurse, activate the EMS chain).
4. Identify causes and signs of choking and describe response within scope for a conscious choking adult.
5. Match common facility emergency codes to the appropriate response.

## Required Learning Content Sections
- Signs/symptoms of distress and prevention.
- Immediate interventions in a medical emergency (within CNA scope).
- Choking/airway obstruction: causes, signs, abdominal thrusts concept (knowledge level).
- Common LTC emergency codes and the chain of response.
- DNR/advance-directive awareness.

## Fictional / De-identified Resident Scenario (S-M12)
*Resident C (fictional)* is coughing forcefully during lunch, then suddenly stops coughing, cannot speak, and clutches the throat. **Decision points:** (1) What is the CNA's immediate scope-safe response and escalation? (2) When are the nurse/EMS summoned? (3) What must be documented afterward? **Scope guardrail + disclaimer:** the scenario teaches recognition/escalation; it does **not** substitute for hands-on CPR/first-aid certification.

## Assessment Items (seed)
1. (MCQ) A resident reports shortness of breath and chest tightness after walking. FIRST CNA action? A) Reassure they'll be fine B) Take vitals first C) Stay and call the nurse immediately D) Call family
2. (TF) Completing this online module certifies the CNA in hands-on CPR.
3. (MCQ) A conscious adult is choking and cannot speak/cough. The CNA should: A) Offer water B) Activate help and respond per training within scope C) Wait and watch D) Leave to find supplies
4. (Matching) Match common facility codes to their meaning/response.
