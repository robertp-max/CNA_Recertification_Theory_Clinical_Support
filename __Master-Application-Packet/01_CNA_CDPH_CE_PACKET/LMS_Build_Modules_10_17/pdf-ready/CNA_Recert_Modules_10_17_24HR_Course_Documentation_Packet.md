## 1. Course Overview

This course delivers **24 online CE hours as 12 units of 2 online CE hours each** for the California CNA two-year renewal cycle, built in Moodle. Content is drawn from the source CCCCO Nurse Assistant Model Curriculum, Modules 10-17, preserved as the source backbone and mapped under each unit. It is delivered at a cognitive/observation/reporting level appropriate to CNA scope of practice. It is **online CE only**: it does not provide, simulate, or validate hands-on clinical skills, does not award clinical hours, does not expand CNA scope, and does not by itself complete all California CNA renewal requirements.

## 5. LMS Delivery Model

- **Platform:** Moodle. Format: Topics/Flexible with Year 1 and Year 2 section groups; 12 unit sections U01-U12.
- **Per unit:** Objectives Page -> Lesson (branching) -> H5P interactive -> fictional Scenario -> Knowledge Check (formative) -> Documentation/Reflection.
- **Completion tracking** enabled at activity, unit, section (Year), and course levels.
- **Restrict access:** Section 0 gates Year 1; within a unit, scenario -> quiz are sequenced; Year 1 completion (and/or date) gates Year 2.
- **Mobile-first, WCAG 2.1 AA** accessibility throughout.

## 6. Assessment Strategy

- **Formative knowledge checks** per unit (KC-U01..KC-U12; 8-10 items, unlimited attempts, per-item feedback, completion-gated).
- **Scenario-based judgment** per unit (S-U01..S-U12; branching, scope guardrails, fictional residents).
- **Summative Year 1 and Year 2 assessments** from unit question banks, **80% pass**, limited attempts, shuffled, review restricted (no answer key exposed).
- **No learner-facing answer key.** Internal answer keys kept separately.

## 7. Certificate Gate Logic

Certificate (Moodle custom certificate) is restricted behind course completion and issues only when ALL gate items pass: identity confirmed; no-PHI agreement; Year 1 units (U01-U06) complete; Year 1 assessment >= 80%; Year 2 units (U07-U12) complete; Year 2 assessment >= 80%; seat-time evidence; learner attestation/affidavit; **and approval status confirmed**. Until approval is confirmed, the certificate condition remains OFF and the course stays Draft.

## 8. Learner Attestation / Affidavit Workflow

1. Enrollment attestation (Section 0): identity + academic integrity + no-PHI agreement.
2. Per-Year integrity attestation before each Year assessment.
3. Final affidavit before certificate: personal completion, genuine engagement time, understanding this is online CE only (no clinical hours) and does not by itself complete all CNA renewal requirements, and no real resident/facility data used.

**Affidavit template (Draft):** "I, [Learner Legal Name], [CNA Certificate No.: ______ if required], attest that I personally completed all online CE activities; that the time recorded reflects my genuine engagement; that I understand this course provides online CE hours only and does not include clinical hours and does not by itself complete all CNA renewal requirements; and that I did not enter any real resident, patient, or facility information. Signature: ______ Date: ______."

## 9. Completion / Evidence Model

A learner completes a unit when all activity-completion conditions are met (lessons viewed with minimum time, scenario completed, knowledge check attempted, documentation submitted). Year completion requires all its units + the Year assessment passed. Course completion requires both Years + final attestation. Evidence: completion reports, time logs, attempt records, scenario logs, attestations, and (post-approval) certificate records.

## 10. Recordkeeping Model

Maintain per learner: enrollment/identity record, unit-completion data (U01-U12), time/log data, assessment attempts/scores, attestations/affidavit, evaluation, and certificate record (post-approval). Retention period **[final verification - longer of CDPH and BPPE requirements]**. Stored in the LMS with secured backups; access restricted; exportable as CSV/PDF for audit.

## 11. No-PHI / No-Real-Resident-Information Policy

No real resident names, facility names, medical record numbers, dates of birth, or real clinical records appear anywhere. All scenarios use fictional, de-identified persons (e.g., "Resident A"). A no-PHI agreement is required in Section 0; a standing banner appears on every free-text/upload activity. Administrators periodically review free-text submissions for inadvertent PHI.

## 12. CNA Scope-of-Practice Policy

Every unit's scenario, knowledge check, and exercise reinforces CNA scope: observe, assist, report to the licensed nurse, and document within scope. CNAs do **not** diagnose, interpret results clinically, change care plans, medicate, or perform tasks reserved for licensed staff. The course does not expand scope and does not grant any hands-on clinical certification.
