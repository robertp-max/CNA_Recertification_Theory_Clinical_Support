## 1. Course Overview

This course converts the approved/source CCCCO Modules 10–17 into a 24-hour online continuing education program for the California CNA two-year renewal cycle, delivered in Moodle. It preserves the eight source modules exactly (titles, statements of purpose, performance objectives) and delivers them at a cognitive/observation/reporting level appropriate to CNA scope of practice. It is **online CE only**: it does not provide, simulate, or validate hands-on clinical skills, does not award clinical hours, does not expand CNA scope, and does not by itself complete all California CNA renewal requirements.

## 2. 24-Hour Calculation

- Convention: **1 online CE hour = 60 minutes** of documented engaged seat time (conservative).
- Allocation: the master application packet contains no official Modules 10–17 hour allocation (its official allocation is a separate 12-Unit × 2-hour structure). A defensible draft allocation is therefore used: **3 online CE hours × 8 modules = 24 online CE hours = 1,440 minutes.**
- This even allocation is a CE **design** normalization and is intentionally different from the uneven CCCCO initial-training theory hours (flagged for confirmation in Section 16).

## 3. Year 1 / Year 2 Split

- **Year 1 (12 CE hours):** Modules 10 Vital Signs, 11 Nutrition, 12 Emergency Procedures, 13 Long Term Care Patient/Resident.

## 5. LMS Delivery Model

- **Platform:** Moodle. Format: Topics/Flexible with Year 1 and Year 2 section groups.
- **Per module:** Objectives Page → Lesson (branching) → H5P interactive → fictional Scenario → Knowledge Check (formative) → Documentation exercise (Assignment) → Reflection.
- **Completion tracking** enabled at activity, module, section (Year), and course levels.
- **Restrict access:** Section 0 (orientation/identity/no-PHI/attestation) gates Year 1; within a module, scenario→quiz→assessment are sequenced; Year 1 completion (and/or date) gates Year 2.
- **Mobile-first, WCAG 2.1 AA** accessibility throughout.

## 6. Assessment Strategy

- **Formative knowledge checks** per module (8–10 items, unlimited attempts, per-item feedback, completion-gated).
- **Scenario-based judgment** per module (branching, scope guardrails in every branch, fictional residents).
- **Summative Year 1 and Year 2 assessments** (≈24 items each from question banks), **80% pass**, limited attempts, shuffled, review options restricted so no answer key is exposed.
- **Remediation** on fail = objective-mapped module review before re-attempt.
- **No learner-facing answer key.** Internal answer keys kept separately (workbook + each module PDF appendix).
- Optional cumulative final only if the master packet requires one (Needs confirmation).

## 7. Certificate Gate Logic

Certificate (Moodle custom certificate) is restricted behind course completion and issues only when ALL gate items pass: identity confirmed; no-PHI agreement; Year 1 modules complete; Year 1 assessment ≥ 80%; Year 2 modules complete; Year 2 assessment ≥ 80%; seat-time evidence; learner attestation/affidavit; **and approval status confirmed**. Until approval is confirmed, the certificate condition remains OFF and the course stays Draft.

## 8. Learner Attestation / Affidavit Workflow

1. Enrollment attestation (Section 0): identity + academic integrity + no-PHI agreement.
2. Per-Year integrity attestation before each Year assessment.
3. Final affidavit before certificate: personal completion, genuine engagement time, understanding that this is online CE only (no clinical hours), and no real resident/facility data used.
Attestations are stored as timestamped submissions and included in the audit export.

**Affidavit template (Draft):** "I, [Learner Legal Name], [CNA Certificate No.: ______ if required], attest that I personally completed all online CE activities; that the time recorded reflects my genuine engagement; that I understand this course provides online CE hours only and does not include clinical hours and does not by itself complete all CNA renewal requirements; and that I did not enter any real resident, patient, or facility information. Signature: ______ Date: ______."

## 9. Completion / Evidence Model

A learner completes a module when all activity-completion conditions are met (lessons viewed with minimum time, scenario completed, knowledge check attempted, documentation submitted). Year completion requires all its modules + the Year assessment passed. Course completion requires both Years + final attestation. Evidence: completion reports, time logs, attempt records, scenario logs, attestations, and (post-approval) certificate records.

## 10. Recordkeeping Model

Maintain per learner: enrollment/identity record, completion data, time/log data, assessment attempts/scores, attestations/affidavit, evaluation, and certificate record (post-approval). Retention period **[Needs confirmation — longer of CDPH and BPPE requirements]**. Stored in the LMS with secured backups; access restricted to authorized administrators/faculty; exportable as CSV/PDF for audit.

## 11. No-PHI / No-Real-Resident-Information Policy

No real resident names, real facility names, real medical record numbers, dates of birth, or real clinical records appear anywhere. All scenarios use fictional, de-identified persons (e.g., "Resident A"). A no-PHI agreement is required in Section 0, and a standing banner appears on every free-text/upload activity: "Use fictional data only. Do not enter real resident, patient, or facility information." Administrators periodically review free-text submissions for inadvertent PHI.

## 12. CNA Scope-of-Practice Policy

Every scenario, knowledge check, and exercise reinforces CNA scope: observe, assist, report to the licensed nurse, and document within scope. CNAs do **not** diagnose, interpret results clinically, change care plans, medicate, or perform tasks reserved for licensed staff. The course does not expand scope and does not grant any hands-on clinical certification.
