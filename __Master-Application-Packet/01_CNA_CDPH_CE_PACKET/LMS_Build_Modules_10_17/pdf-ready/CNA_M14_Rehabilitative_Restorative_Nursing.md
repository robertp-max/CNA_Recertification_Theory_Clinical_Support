## Module Description
Introduces restorative care: helping each resident reach his/her optimal level of functioning and maximum independent-living skills through rehabilitative/restorative procedures, the rehab team, ADLs, ROM, adaptive/comfort devices, prevention of complications of inactivity, and the CNA's documentation/care-plan role.

## Measurable Learning Objectives
The learner will be able to:
1. Define key rehab/restorative terminology (ADLs, ROM, contractures, atrophy, prosthesis, ambulation, decubitus/pressure ulcer).
2. Explain restorative-care philosophy and how it promotes independence and resident potential.
3. State the goals of restorative care.
4. Describe the rehab team and the CNA's scope-safe role on it.
5. List ADLs and CNA approaches that promote resident self-care.
6. Identify common comfort/adaptive devices and their purposes.
7. Describe ROM types and steps the CNA takes to prevent complications of inactivity (observe/assist/report); describe the CNA's documentation and care-plan-meeting role.

## Required Learning Content Sections
- Restorative-care philosophy and goals.
- The rehabilitation team and CNA role.
- Promoting self-care; ADLs.
- Comfort and adaptive devices.

## Fictional / De-identified Resident Scenario (S-M14)
*Resident E (fictional)*, recovering from a hip procedure, asks the CNA to "just do it all for me." **Decision points:** (1) What restorative approach best promotes independence? (2) How does the CNA balance safety and self-care? (3) What is documented for the care plan? **Scope guardrail:** assist only as needed, encourage independence within the care plan, observe/report — no independent therapy decisions.

## Assessment Items (seed)
1. (MCQ) Restorative care for a resident who can do part of an ADL means the CNA should: A) Do everything for them B) Assist only as needed and encourage independence C) Skip the ADL D) Rush them
2. (Matching) Match adaptive/comfort device (trochanter roll, foot board, bed cradle, heel protector) to its purpose.
3. (MCQ) Passive ROM means: A) Resident performs it alone B) The CNA moves the joint for a resident who cannot C) No movement D) Resistance training
4. (TF) A CNA may independently increase a resident's ROM program beyond the care plan.
