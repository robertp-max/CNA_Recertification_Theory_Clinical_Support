## Module Description
Prepares the CNA to know how, when, and why vital signs are taken and how to recognize, report, and record normal and abnormal findings within CNA scope. Covers temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure, plus pain as an observation. **This is online CE only and does not validate hands-on vital-signs measurement competency.**

## Measurable Learning Objectives
The learner will be able to:
1. Define key vital-signs terminology (febrile, afebrile, tachycardia, bradycardia, dyspnea, apnea, systolic, diastolic, hypertension, hypotension).
2. State adult normal ranges for temperature (oral/rectal/axillary/tympanic/temporal), pulse, respiration, and blood pressure.
3. Identify factors that increase or decrease each vital sign.
4. Distinguish normal from abnormal findings that must be reported to the licensed nurse.
5. Describe the correct order and rationale for measuring TPR as a combined process.
6. Identify objective signs of pain and scope-safe pain-observation/reporting steps.
7. Record vital signs accurately (T, P, R order; BP as systolic/diastolic; note non-oral/non-arm sites) within CNA documentation scope.

## Required Learning Content Sections
- Definition and purpose of vital signs; observations made while taking them (skin color/temperature, behavior, comfort).
- Temperature: routes, normal ranges, factors raising/lowering, measures to raise/lower body temperature.
- Pulse: circulation basics, sites, adult range (60–100), factors, qualities (rate/rhythm/strength).
- Respiration: definition, adult range (12–20), abnormal patterns (dyspnea, apnea, Cheyne-Stokes, orthopnea, tachypnea, bradypnea).
- TPR as a combined procedure (order and rationale).
- Blood pressure: systolic/diastolic concepts, normal/abnormal ranges, equipment, factors, scope-safe cautions (no BP on injured/IV/dialysis/mastectomy side).
- Pain observation and reporting (pain not a normal part of aging; objective signs; pain scales as a facility tool).
- Recording vitals on flow sheets/graphic records/NA notes per facility policy.

## Fictional / De-identified Resident Scenario (S-M10)
*Resident A (fictional)* returns to the room after a short walk and reports shortness of breath and tightness in the chest. **Decision points:** (1) What does the CNA do first? (2) What is within CNA scope vs. the nurse's role? (3) What is documented? **Scope guardrail:** the CNA stays with the resident, calls the nurse/activates help immediately, observes and reports — does not diagnose or medicate.

## Assessment Items (seed — see Internal Answer Key Appendix)
1. (MCQ) Which adult finding should the CNA report as abnormal? A) Pulse 72 B) Resp 16 C) Pulse 124 D) Oral temp 98.6°F
2. (TF) A CNA may adjust a resident's blood pressure medication if the reading is high.
3. (MCQ) Correct order for recording vital signs: A) P,T,R B) BP,R,T C) T,P,R D) R,P,BP
4. (MCQ) Difficult or labored breathing is called: A) Tachypnea B) Apnea C) Dyspnea D) Bradypnea

## Internal Answer Key Appendix (admin/faculty only — do NOT publish to learners)
1 = C (tachycardia >100). 2 = False (out of scope; observe/report only). 3 = C (T,P,R). 4 = C (dyspnea).
