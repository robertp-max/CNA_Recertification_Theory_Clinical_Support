## Module Description
Introduces patient/resident abuse with a focus on the CNA's role in preventing, recognizing, and reporting abuse, including types of abuse, contributing issues, resident rights, mandated-reporter duty, and reporting pathways.

## Measurable Learning Objectives
The learner will be able to:
1. Define key abuse terminology (abuse, neglect, negligence, assault, battery, false imprisonment, involuntary seclusion, mandated reporter, ombudsman, financial/physical/psychological/sexual/verbal abuse).
2. Identify and describe types of abuse.
3. Discuss issues and contributing factors related to elder abuse.
4. Explain the CNA's role in preventing abuse and protecting resident rights.
5. Describe the CNA's mandated-reporter role and the correct reporting pathways and timelines.

## Required Learning Content Sections
- Types of abuse and key legal/ethical terms.
- Issues and contributing factors.
- Prevention and resident rights (restraints, involuntary seclusion, privacy, confidentiality/HIPAA basics).
- Recognizing and reporting abuse; mandated-reporter duty; ombudsman/supervisor/state channels.

## Key Terms
Abuse, neglect, negligence, assault, battery, false imprisonment, involuntary seclusion, coercion, defamation (libel/slander), aiding and abetting, financial abuse, physical abuse, psychological abuse, sexual abuse, verbal abuse, theft; mandated reporter, ombudsman, grievances, resident council, social services; HIPAA, privacy, confidential, informed consent, restraints, scope of practice, ethical/legal standard, policy and procedure.

## Fictional / De-identified Resident Scenario (S-M17)
A fictional CNA observes a coworker handling *Resident G (fictional)* roughly and threatening them. **Decision points:** (1) What is the CNA's immediate duty to the resident's safety? (2) As a mandated reporter, what reporting steps and timelines apply? (3) What confidentiality obligations apply? **Scope guardrail:** ensure resident safety, report immediately per policy and to required authorities, document objectively — do not ignore, delay, or only "handle it privately."

## Assessment Items (seed)
1. (MCQ) A CNA witnesses a coworker handling a resident roughly and threatening them. The CNA must: A) Say nothing B) Ensure resident safety and report immediately per policy/mandated-reporter duty C) Only confront the coworker D) Wait a week
2. (MCQ) Which is an example of involuntary seclusion / a rights violation? A) Offering activities B) Confining a resident against their will as punishment C) Assisting to the dining room D) Honoring a preference
3. (TF) A CNA is a mandated reporter of suspected abuse.
4. (MCQ) Resident records and abuse reports must be kept: A) Public B) Confidential and shared only through proper channels C) On social media D) With other residents
