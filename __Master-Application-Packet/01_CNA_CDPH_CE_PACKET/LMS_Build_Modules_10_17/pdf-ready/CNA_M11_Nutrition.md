## Module Description
Examines the body's need for food and fluids and the CNA's role in helping residents meet nutrition and hydration needs: nutrients and food sources, common therapeutic diets, safe feeding assistance, hydration, cultural/religious dietary respect, and awareness of alternative nutrition routes (observe/report only).

## Measurable Learning Objectives
The learner will be able to:
1. Define key nutrition terminology (dysphagia, aspiration, NPO, therapeutic diet, I&O, dehydration, edema, pocketing).
2. Explain the body's need for food and fluids and identify signs of dehydration/over-hydration to report.
3. List common nutrients and their food sources.
4. Match common therapeutic diets (clear/full liquid, pureed, mechanical soft, low-sodium, low-fat, bland, thickened-liquid) to the CNA's role.
5. Describe safe feeding-assistance techniques and aspiration-prevention precautions (positioning, pacing, supervision).
6. Describe how cultural and religious preferences affect dietary care and how to respect them.
7. Identify alternative nutrition routes (NG, PEG, TPN) as observe-and-report-only for the CNA.

## Required Learning Content Sections
- Body's need for food/fluids; consequences of poor intake.
- Nutrients and food sources; food-guidance system; vegan four food groups.
- Nutritional and fluid needs of the elderly; hydration; intake & output.
- Therapeutic diets and CNA responsibilities.
- Safe feeding techniques; dysphagia/aspiration precautions.
- Cultural and religious influences on diet.
- Alternative ways nutrition is administered (awareness; CNA observes/reports).

## Fictional / De-identified Resident Scenario (S-M11)
*Resident B (fictional)* is ordered a pureed diet with thickened liquids. A meal tray arrives with a regular sandwich and thin juice. **Decision points:** (1) Should the CNA serve it? (2) What scope-safe action prevents harm? (3) What is reported and to whom? **Scope guardrail:** withhold, verify the diet order with the nurse, and report — aspiration risk; the CNA does not modify the diet order.

## Assessment Items (seed)
1. (MCQ) A resident on a thickened-liquid diet is served thin juice. The CNA should: A) Serve it B) Withhold and verify the order with the nurse C) Thin it further D) Ignore it
2. (Matching) Match pureed / clear liquid / low-sodium / mechanical soft to the correct description.
3. (MCQ) Which is a sign of dehydration the CNA reports? A) Moist mucous membranes B) Dark concentrated urine and dry mouth C) Normal skin turgor D) Steady weight
4. (TF) A CNA may insert or adjust a feeding (NG/PEG) tube.
