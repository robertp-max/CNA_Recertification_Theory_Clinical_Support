## Module Description
Introduces the effects of aging on body structure and function, common physical and psychological conditions of elderly residents, dementia-sensitive care approaches, community resources, and a basic anatomy/physiology and body-systems overview, with the complications of immobility a CNA observes and reports.

> **Hour note:** the source distinguishes SNF/ICF (5 theory hours) vs non-SNF/ICF (3 hours + 2 additional). The applicable variant must be confirmed (see full packet, Section 17).

## Measurable Learning Objectives
The learner will be able to:
1. Define key LTC terminology (chronic, acute, dementia, reality orientation, validation therapy, contractures, subacute care).
2. Describe common human needs of the elderly resident (environmental, psychological, social, recreational, spiritual) and scope-safe CNA interventions.
3. Identify community resources that support elderly residents.
4. Describe dementia-sensitive care approaches and scope-safe de-escalation.
5. Describe special needs of residents with developmental/mental disorders (e.g., intellectual disability, cerebral palsy, epilepsy, Parkinson's, mental illness).
6. Summarize basic body organization, aging changes by body system, and the complications of immobility the CNA observes/reports.

## Required Learning Content Sections

## Fictional / De-identified Resident Scenario (S-M13)
*Resident D (fictional)*, who has dementia, becomes increasingly agitated and resistant during evening care (sundowning). **Decision points:** (1) Which scope-safe, dignity-preserving approaches reduce distress? (2) What should the CNA avoid? (3) What objective observation is reported? **Scope guardrail:** calm, simple, reassuring approaches; reduce stimulation; report behavior changes — no behavioral diagnosis, no restraints.

## Assessment Items (seed)
1. (MCQ) Best scope-safe approach to an agitated resident with dementia? A) Argue with the facts B) Calm, simple, reassuring approach; reduce stimulation C) Apply restraints D) Leave them alone
2. (MCQ) Which is a complication of immobility the CNA observes/reports? A) Pressure injury/skin breakdown B) Improved appetite C) Lower pulse D) Faster healing
3. (TF) A CNA may diagnose a resident with depression based on behavior.
4. (MCQ) Validation therapy for a resident with dementia means: A) Correcting every error B) Acknowledging feelings/reality as the resident experiences them C) Ignoring them D) Restraining them

## Internal Answer Key Appendix (admin/faculty only — do NOT publish)
1 = B. 2 = A. 3 = False (out of scope; observe/report). 4 = B.
