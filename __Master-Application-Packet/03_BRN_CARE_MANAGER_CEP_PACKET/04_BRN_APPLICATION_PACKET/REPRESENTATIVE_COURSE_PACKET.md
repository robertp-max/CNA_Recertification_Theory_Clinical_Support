## Course identity

- Course title: RN Case Management Foundations: Care Coordination, Transitions, Documentation, and Ethical Practice
- Target audience: Registered Nurses and RN-supervised care-management staff
- RN relevance: Directly supports indirect patient/client care, care coordination, transition planning, documentation quality, and nurse-led interdisciplinary communication
- Contact-hour value: 4 contact hours
- Contact-hour calculation: 4 contact hours x 50 instructional minutes each = 200 instructional minutes
- Delivery type: `[[REGULATOR VERIFICATION REQUIRED]]` Live webinar or instructor-led online session to be confirmed by signer

## Behavioral objectives

- Apply nurse-led care-coordination principles during transitions of care and discharge planning.
- Distinguish high-risk documentation failures that affect continuity of care, safety, and reimbursement-sensitive coordination work.
- Use interdisciplinary communication strategies that keep the RN role tied to patient/client care planning.
- Identify ethical and scope-safe escalation points for medication reconciliation, psychosocial barriers, and social-determinant concerns.

## Teaching methods
