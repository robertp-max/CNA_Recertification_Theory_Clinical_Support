# Representative BRN Course Packet

Status: `[[SME REVIEW REQUIRED BEFORE SUBMISSION]]`

## Course identity

- Course title: RN Case Management Foundations: Care Coordination, Transitions, Documentation, and Ethical Practice
- Target audience: Registered Nurses and RN-supervised care-management staff
- RN / case-management relevance: Directly tied to indirect patient/client care, care coordination, transitions of care, documentation quality, and nurse-led interdisciplinary communication
- Contact-hour value: 4 contact hours
- Contact-hour calculation: 4 contact hours x 50 instructional minutes = 200 instructional minutes
- Delivery type: Live webinar / live-stream instructor-led session

## Behavioral objectives

- Apply nurse-led care-coordination principles during transitions of care and discharge planning.
- Distinguish documentation failures that affect continuity of care, patient/client safety, and care-management quality.
- Use interdisciplinary communication strategies that keep the RN role tied to direct or indirect patient/client care.
- Identify ethical and scope-safe escalation points for medication reconciliation, psychosocial barriers, and social-determinant concerns.

## Timed outline

| Segment | Minutes | Content |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 50 | Foundations of RN case management, transitions of care, and scope-safe coordination duties |
| 2 | 50 | Documentation quality, care-plan updates, communication handoffs, and patient/client safety triggers |
| 3 | 50 | Medication-reconciliation coordination, chronic-condition support, and interdisciplinary collaboration |
| 4 | 50 | Ethical issues, social determinants, escalation scenarios, and application review |

## Teaching methods

- Instructor-led lecture with scenario-based discussion
- Guided case review using care-coordination examples
- Polling or verbal knowledge checks during each segment
- End-of-course evaluation and completion attestation

## Evaluation method

Participants complete a course evaluation covering objective clarity, relevance to RN practice, applicability to current work, instructor mastery, and suggestions for improvement.

## Assessment / completion method

- Full attendance for the approved contact-hour duration
- Active participation in at least one check-in per segment
- Completion attestation and end-of-course evaluation

## Instructor requirements

- Lead instructor must be a currently licensed RN with recent case-management, care-coordination, discharge-planning, utilization-review, or comparable nursing-practice experience.
