## A. Executive Summary

This package is a build-ready blueprint for one California-BRN-renewal-compatible continuing education course for registered nurses working in care/case management. It is sized at **30 contact hours**, organized into **10 modules of 3 contact hours each**, and engineered for delivery in **Moodle** with auditable completion evidence.

- **Contact-hour math (draft):** 30 contact hours × 50 instructional minutes = **1,500 instructional minutes minimum**. Each module = 3 contact hours = **≥150 instructional minutes**. No module is under 60 minutes. This is design math only, **not** an approval claim.
- **BRN fit posture:** Every module is mapped to a *direct or indirect patient/client-care* rationale grounded in the practice of registered nursing (care coordination, assessment, documentation, advocacy, transitions, utilization review, quality/risk, chronic/geriatric management). See Section C.
- **Primary deliverable:** this markdown course package. The Excel workbook (`BRN_CM_CE_LMS_BUILD_WORKBOOK.xlsx`) is a **support/defensibility artifact only** (Section Q).

- **Source of truth:** Provider identity, instructor of record, policies, certificate elements, and fee are taken from the master packet. Course-design content (modules, lessons, assessments) is **new draft instructional design** and is marked for SME/compliance review throughout.

## C. BRN Fit Rationale

**Standard applied:** BRN CE content must be relevant to the practice of registered nursing and related to scientific knowledge, technical skills, or direct/indirect patient/client care. Nursing administration/management, quality assurance, legal aspects of nursing, current healthcare trends, care coordination, patient advocacy, documentation, discharge planning, and utilization/risk management qualify when **clearly tied to nursing practice**. This course ties every module to that standard.

| Module | Direct or indirect patient care | One-line nursing-practice anchor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Foundations & Scope | Indirect | Defines RN scope/role boundaries that govern safe nurse-led case management. |
| 2. Assessment, Intake & Care Planning | Direct | Nursing assessment and patient-centered care planning are core RN practice. |
| 3. Care Coordination Across Disciplines/Settings | Direct + Indirect | Nurse-led interdisciplinary coordination protects continuity of patient care. |
| 4. Documentation & Legal Charting | Indirect | Accurate nursing documentation is a legal/clinical patient-safety function. |
| 5. Patient Rights, Consent, Advocacy, Ethics | Direct | Advocacy, informed consent, ethical boundaries are direct RN duties. |
| 6. Transitions & Discharge Planning | Direct | Discharge planning prevents readmission harm — direct patient care. |
| 7. Utilization Review & Medical Necessity | Indirect | UR tied to nursing judgment protects appropriate, safe patient care. |
| 8. Quality, Risk, Incident Reporting, Escalation | Indirect | QA/risk/escalation are nursing patient-safety and legal-aspects content. |
| 9. Chronic, Geriatric & High-Risk Management | Direct | Applies scientific knowledge to high-risk patient populations. |
| 10. Integrated Capstone & Final Assessment | Direct + Indirect | Synthesizes nurse-led care management across the patient journey. |

**Defensibility note:** Modules 7 and 8 (UR / QA-risk) are the highest-scrutiny items because they border administrative content. Each is written to keep the **nursing-judgment and patient-safety thread** explicit so it reads as indirect patient care, not pure business administration. `[[SME REVIEW REQUIRED BEFORE SUBMISSION]]`

### CM-M01 — Foundations of RN Case Management and Nursing Scope
- **Contact hours / est. minutes:** 3 / 165
- **BRN content rationale (indirect patient care):** Establishes the RN scope-of-practice and role boundaries that make nurse-led case management safe and lawful; frames case management as a function within registered-nursing practice rather than a separate license.
- **Learning objectives (measurable):** By the end of the module the learner will be able to:
  1. Define RN case management and distinguish it from social work and unlicensed care coordination, using BRN scope language.
  2. Identify three RN scope-of-practice boundaries that constrain case-management decisions.
  3. Describe how care coordination qualifies as direct/indirect patient care under nursing practice.
  4. Recognize when a case-management task must be escalated to a licensed prescriber or RN supervisor.
- **Lesson breakdown:**
  - L1 What RN case management is (and is not) — 35 min
  - L2 Nursing scope of practice and legal boundaries — 40 min
  - L3 Roles on the care team; RN vs. SW vs. unlicensed staff — 35 min
  - L4 The case-management process model (screen → assess → plan → coordinate → monitor → evaluate) — 35 min
  - Knowledge check + reflection — 20 min
- **LMS activity sequence:** Page (overview) → Book/SCORM lesson (L1–L4) → Quiz (KC1) → Assignment (reflection) → Activity completion checkpoint.
- **Knowledge checks (low-stakes):** 8-item KC1 (MCQ + true/false), unlimited attempts, ≥80% to complete; immediate per-item feedback, no full key.
- **Case scenario (fictional):** "Nurse R receives a home-health referral for a fictional 72-year-old client recovering from a hip procedure. The referral asks Nurse R to 'adjust the pain medication.'" Learner identifies the scope boundary and the correct escalation path.
- **Documentation/decision exercise:** Learner writes a 5-line scope-and-escalation note documenting why the medication change is out of RN scope and who it was routed to.
- **Completion requirement:** All lessons viewed + KC1 ≥80% + reflection submitted.
- **Evidence artifact:** Activity-completion log, KC1 score, reflection text.
- **SME/compliance flags:** Confirm BRN scope phrasing with RN SME (Vanessa Valerio). `[[SME REVIEW REQUIRED]]`
- **PHI-safety notes:** Scenario fully fictional; no names, MRNs, dates of service, or facilities.
- **Accessibility notes:** Captioned video, text transcript, semantic headings, no color-only cues, alt text on diagrams.
- **Suggested Moodle activity types:** Page, Book, Quiz, Assignment, Label.

- Lessons/Books: "require view." Quizzes: "require passing grade (≥80%)." Assignments: "require submission" (+ "require grade" where faculty-scored).
- Course completion = all 10 module checkpoints + final exam passed + evaluation submitted.

**Restrict access rules (sequential gating)**
- Each module section restricted by completion of the prior module (Restrict access → activity completion).
- Final exam restricted until Modules 1–9 complete and capstone submitted.
- Certificate restricted until course completion criteria met (Section J).

**Quiz settings**
- KCs: unlimited attempts, highest grade, immediate feedback, **no review of correct answers after close**, question + answer shuffling, ≥80% to complete.
- Final exam: limited attempts (recommend 3), question bank random draw, shuffle, **no review showing the full answer key**, time limit on, ≥80% to pass.

**Attempt settings**
- KC remediation: after a failed attempt, show per-item feedback + a "review this lesson" link, then allow retry.
- Final exam: after each failed attempt, route to a remediation page (Section G) before next attempt; lock after max attempts → manual faculty review.

**Passing threshold**
- 80% on all graded knowledge checks and the final exam; capstone must meet rubric minimum.

**Gradebook structure (categories)**
- Knowledge Checks (10 items, low weight), Applied Exercises/Assignments (10 items), Capstone (rubric), Final Exam (gate). Course total configured so certificate logic reads "complete + passed."

**Certificate gate conditions** — see Section J.

**Activity completion conditions** — per-item as above; every module ends in a visible completion checkpoint (Label/Page) so learners see progress.

**Time / participation evidence strategy**

**Mobile-first design notes**

**Accessibility requirements (WCAG 2.1 AA target)**
