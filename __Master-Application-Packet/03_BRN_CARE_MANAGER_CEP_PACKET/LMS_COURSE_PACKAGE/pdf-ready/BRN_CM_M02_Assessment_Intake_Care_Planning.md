### BRN Relevance Rationale

Nursing assessment, problem identification, and patient-centered goal setting are core RN clinical practice and the foundation of safe care plans.

### Module Description

Builds skill in structured intake, distinguishing subjective/objective data, screening social determinants of health, and writing measurable, patient-centered goals while checking for implicit bias.

### Measurable Learning Objectives

By the end of this module the learner will be able to:

1. Conduct a structured intake using a standardized nursing assessment framework.
2. Differentiate subjective and objective data and document each appropriately.
3. Write at least two measurable, patient-centered goals from assessment findings.
4. Identify social determinants of health and screen for implicit bias in intake judgments.

### Required Readings / Learning Content Sections

- Reading 2.2 - Nursing assessment frameworks and SDOH screening.

### Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Subjective data | Information reported by the patient (e.g., 'I feel tired'). |
| Objective data | Measurable/observable findings (e.g., blood pressure, observed gait). |
| SDOH | Social determinants of health - conditions affecting health (housing, transport, income). |
| Patient-centered goal | A measurable outcome defined with the patient's values and priorities. |
| Implicit bias | Unconscious attitudes that can affect clinical judgment and equity. |

### Fictional / De-identified Case Scenario

*All clinical content is fictional and de-identified. No PHI.* A fictional new home-health client with type 2 diabetes reports difficulty attending appointments because of limited transportation and lives alone.

### Learner Activities

- Read Sections 2.1-2.4.
- Sort sample data points into subjective vs. objective.
- Identify the SDOH barrier in the scenario and a nursing intervention.
- Complete the Care-Plan Worksheet and Knowledge Check 2.

### Documentation / Decision-Making Exercise

Complete the Care-Plan Worksheet (template provided): list 3 nursing problems, 2 measurable patient-centered goals, 1 SDOH intervention, and a one-line implicit-bias check note.

### Knowledge Check Blueprint

10 items (MCQ + multi-select, scenario-tagged), unlimited attempts, immediate feedback, no full key, >=80% to complete.

### Assessment Items (Learner-Facing Stems)

**KC2-Q1 (Multi-select).** Which are examples of objective assessment data? (select all that apply)
- A) Reported pain 7/10
- B) Blood pressure reading
- C) Observed unsteady gait
- D) 'I feel tired'

**KC2-Q2 (MCQ).** Which is the best-written measurable, patient-centered goal?
- A) Patient will be healthier
- B) Patient will check blood glucose twice daily and log results for 2 weeks
- C) Patient will try harder
- D) Nurse will fix the diabetes

**KC2-Q3 (MCQ).** The transportation barrier in the scenario is best classified as:
- A) A medication error
- B) A social determinant of health
- C) Objective vital sign
- D) A billing issue
