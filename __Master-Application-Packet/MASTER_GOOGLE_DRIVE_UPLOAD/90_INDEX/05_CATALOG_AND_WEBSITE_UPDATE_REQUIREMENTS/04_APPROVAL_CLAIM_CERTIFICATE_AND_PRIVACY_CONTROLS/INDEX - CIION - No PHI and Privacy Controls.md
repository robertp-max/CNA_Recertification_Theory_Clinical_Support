# 16 - No-PHI and Privacy Controls

Status: Draft / Pending Approval. Companion learner-facing copy: `generated_missing_elements/GENERATED_NO_PHI_LEARNER_WARNINGS.md`.

## Learner warning text
- A prominent no-PHI warning is presented at course start and before any open-text/submission activity (exact copy in the generated file). Learners must acknowledge before proceeding.

## Activity prompt rules
- All scenario, documentation, and discussion prompts instruct learners to use only fictional, de-identified examples. Prompts explicitly forbid real names, dates of birth, record numbers, addresses, or other identifiers.

## No upload of real charts
- Upload activities (if any) prohibit uploading real patient/resident charts, photos, or documents. Provide fictional templates instead. Where possible, restrict file types and include the warning at the upload point.

## Fictional scenarios only
- All built-in scenarios and quiz items use fictional, de-identified content (no real resident/patient names). The question banks were authored to this rule.

## Support escalation if PHI is entered
- If a learner enters apparent PHI, staff escalate to the designated privacy contact (role-based; named contact = final human verification required) and follow the quarantine/deletion process below.

## Deletion / quarantine process
1. Identify and restrict access to the affected submission/post.
2. Quarantine (hide) the content pending review.
3. Remove/redact the PHI per policy and document the action (who/when/what, without copying the PHI into the log).
4. Notify the privacy contact; assess whether any breach-notification obligation applies (legal/compliance determination required).

## Instructor / admin review responsibility
- Editing Teachers/Managers periodically review open-text submissions for accidental PHI and confirm prompts/warnings are present. Compliance Reviewer spot-checks as part of survey readiness (test PH-02).
