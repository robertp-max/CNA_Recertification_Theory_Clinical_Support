### Page: Course Overview

This course prepares RCFE administrators to identify and apply current California RCFE statutory, regulatory, policy, and procedural standards in daily operations, incident response, documentation, and compliance decision-making.

This Moodle course is built as one filed LIC 9140 course. It is not a module inside the future 40-hour / 12-module concept. It must remain labeled Draft / Pending CDSS/ACS Approval until official approval metadata is available.

Learning objectives:
- Identify the role of Health and Safety Code section 1569 and Title 22, Division 6, Chapter 8 in RCFE operations.
- Differentiate licensing, certification, mandated reporting, complaint, personal rights, and recordkeeping obligations.
- Apply a regulatory cross-check process before implementing a policy or operational change.
- Recognize situations requiring escalation before public claims, certificates, or CDSS submissions are made.

### Page: Lesson Content

#### Hour 1: RCFE legal framework and official-source navigation

Learners map the relationship among statute, regulation, CDSS/CCLD guidance, facility policy, and administrator action. The lesson emphasizes that administrators must confirm requirements from official sources before changing operations or making public-facing statements.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.

#### Hour 2: Title 22 duties, mandated reporting, confidentiality, and complaints

Learners review how regulatory duties become daily controls: confidential resident information is protected, complaints are handled without retaliation, mandated reports are made through approved channels, and rights issues are documented objectively.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.

#### Hour 3: Policies, procedural controls, documentation, and escalation

Learners practice converting a broad requirement into a usable facility procedure. The lesson focuses on policy ownership, review cycles, version control, staff communication, and escalation when interpretation is uncertain.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.

#### Hour 4: Applied regulatory scenarios

Learners work through admissions, incident, record, and marketing-language scenarios. Each scenario requires the administrator to identify the controlling source, decide the next action, document the rationale, and avoid premature approval claims.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.

### H5P/Lesson: Scenario Application

Scenario: A fictional RCFE receives a notice that its complaint poster is outdated and its policy binder still refers to an obsolete procedure. The administrator must correct the posting, update the policy, communicate the change to staff, and document a plan to prevent recurrence.

Administrator activity: Build a one-page regulatory cross-check and corrective-action memo with source checked, risk identified, immediate correction, staff communication, record location, and follow-up date.

Builder instructions: configure this as a Moodle Lesson or H5P Branching Scenario with required completion. Learners must choose an action, receive corrective feedback for unsafe choices, and submit the final documentation product through Assignment or online text.

### Page: Course Overview

This course focuses on dementia-care foundations for RCFE administrators, including person-centered approaches, communication, behavior interpretation, individualized service planning, staff direction, and documentation expectations.

This Moodle course is built as one filed LIC 9140 course. It is not a module inside the future 40-hour / 12-module concept. It must remain labeled Draft / Pending CDSS/ACS Approval until official approval metadata is available.

Learning objectives:
- Describe common dementia-related changes that affect communication, behavior, safety, and care planning.
- Identify non-pharmacologic, person-centered strategies for responding to distress and unmet needs.
- Connect dementia care practices to RCFE assessment, service planning, supervision, and documentation responsibilities.
- Evaluate whether staff instructions align with resident dignity, safety, and regulatory expectations.

### Page: Lesson Content

#### Hour 1: Dementia overview and administrator implications

Learners review common cognitive, functional, behavioral, and communication changes seen in Alzheimer's disease and related dementias. The administrator lens is care planning, staff direction, documentation, and escalation rather than diagnosis.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.

#### Hour 2: Communication, unmet needs, and de-escalation

Learners practice interpreting behavior as communication. The lesson emphasizes approach, validation, cueing, environmental adjustment, pain or discomfort review, and non-pharmacologic responses before escalation.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.

#### Hour 3: Individualized planning, family communication, and staff coaching

Learners connect resident history, routines, preferences, and abilities to needs and services planning. Administrators practice coaching staff in language that preserves dignity and avoids judgmental labels.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.

#### Hour 4: Case application and documentation

Learners apply person-centered dementia foundations to a fictional case involving distress during care. The activity requires resident-centered interventions, staff instructions, family communication boundaries, and documentation.

Learner application: pause after the page content and identify the specific administrator decision, the record that should be created or updated, and the escalation point if the issue cannot be resolved through routine procedure.
