# Generated CNA Unit Objectives and Activities

Status: Draft / Pending Approval. Generated Moodle MVP implementation artifact. SME review required. Online CE only; no clinical hours; fictional/de-identified examples only. Source backbone: CCCCO/NATP Modules 10-17.

Each unit uses the standard activity set: Overview, Objectives, Content, Checkpoint, Unit Quiz, Scenario, Attestation (see course shell spec). Suggested objectives below.

## U01 - Vital Signs I (Module 10)
Objectives: state the purpose of vital signs; identify the four standard vital signs; use correct temperature/pain terminology; record and report values within scope. Activities: overview; objectives; content; checkpoint; quiz (CNA/U01); scenario (observe/report); attestation.

## U02 - Vital Signs II (Module 10)
Objectives: measure pulse, respirations, and blood pressure accurately; recognize reportable changes; record values with time. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U02); scenario (respiratory distress recognition).

## U03 - Nutrition, Hydration, Diets, Feeding, Aspiration (Module 11)
Objectives: describe common therapeutic diet textures; apply safe feeding positioning; recognize/report dysphagia and dehydration signs. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U03); scenario (thickened-liquid order).

## U04 - Emergency Procedures, Distress, Choking, Codes (Module 12)
Objectives: recognize choking severity; apply the facility emergency response sequence; know facility codes and CNA role/scope. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U04); scenario (fire/evacuation within scope).

## U05 - LTC Resident I: Aging, Body Systems, Conditions (Module 13)
Objectives: distinguish normal aging from reportable changes; identify aging-related skin/mobility risks; support independence. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U05); scenario (acute mental status change).

## U06 - LTC Resident II: Dementia-Sensitive, Psychosocial (Module 13)
Objectives: apply dementia-sensitive communication; respond to agitation/wandering with dignity; support psychosocial needs. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U06); scenario (exit-seeking redirection).

## U07 - Rehabilitative Nursing and Restorative Care (Module 14)
Objectives: state restorative-care goals; apply safe ROM/mobility support; reinforce regained skills; use good body mechanics. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U07); scenario (dizziness during activity).

## U08 - Observation and Charting I (Module 15)
Objectives: distinguish objective vs subjective data; report specifically and promptly; prioritize urgent observations. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U08); scenario (clustered observations).

## U09 - Observation and Charting II: Legal Documentation (Module 15)
Objectives: apply accurate/timely charting; correct errors properly; maintain confidentiality; recognize the record as legal. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U09); scenario (late entry).

## U10 - Death and Dying (Module 16)
Objectives: describe hospice/comfort care goals; provide culturally sensitive comfort; respect advance directives; perform respectful postmortem care within scope. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U10); scenario (family prognosis question).

## U11 - Resident Abuse I: Rights, Types, Prevention (Module 17)
Objectives: identify resident rights; recognize abuse/neglect/exploitation types and warning signs; apply prevention. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U11); scenario (witnessed rough handling).

## U12 - Resident Abuse II: Mandated Reporting (Module 17)
Objectives: apply mandated-reporter duties; document objectively; maintain confidentiality; understand good-faith reporting. Activities: standard set; quiz (CNA/U12); scenario (resident discloses exploitation). Note: jurisdiction-specific reporting specifics flagged for SME/legal review.

## FINAL - Comprehensive (Modules 10-17)
Objectives: integrate knowledge across all 12 units; demonstrate reportable-change recognition, documentation integrity, abuse recognition, and scope awareness. Activity: comprehensive final (CNA/FINAL, 50 items), gated behind all units.
