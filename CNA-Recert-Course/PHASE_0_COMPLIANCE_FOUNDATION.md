**Project:** California CNA Recertification Course Compliance Research  
**Intended architecture reviewed:** 12 hours asynchronous theory in Moodle plus 12 hours in-person/workplace/lab/preceptor-supported clinical practice documentation  
**Jurisdiction:** California  
**Prepared for:** CI Institute of Nursing  
**Status:** Research foundation only; not course design; not legal advice

## 1. Executive Summary

**Verified requirement.** California CNA certificates renew every two years. Renewal is conditioned on documented completion of 48 hours of in-service training/continuing education within the two-year certification period, with at least 12 hours completed in each year. California allows only 24 of the 48 hours to be obtained through a CDPH-approved online computer training program. Sources: California Health and Safety Code section 1337.6; CDPH Online Continuing Education Providers page; CDPH 283A; CDPH 283C; 22 CCR section 71839. [S1] [S2] [S3] [S4] [S5]

**Verified requirement.** California renewal also requires the CNA to have provided nursing or nursing-related services in a health care facility to residents for compensation under supervision of a licensed health professional during the most recent certification period. Source: CDPH 283C. [S4]

**Verified requirement.** Online CNA CE is not merely "asynchronous content." CDPH online CE provider materials require identity confirmation, interactive feedback, an exam/test, a final printable signed statement/affidavit before certificate issuance, a time/pause mechanism enforcing at least 50 minutes of active participation per CE hour, certificates containing specified fields, and record retention. Sources: HSC 1337.6; CDPH 192B; CDPH 192. [S1] [S6] [S7]

**Needs state verification.** A 12-hour asynchronous Moodle theory block can fit within California's online CE allowance only if CI Institute of Nursing is a CDPH-approved online CE provider, the specific online courses are approved, the hours are within the learner's 24-hour online limit for the renewal cycle, and all CDPH online CE controls are implemented. The 12 hours should not be marketed as satisfying all California renewal requirements.

**Needs state verification.** California CNA recertification sources reviewed do not establish a separate 12-hour clinical practice requirement for renewal. A 12-hour clinical practice component may be valuable as optional learner support, employer in-service, workplace competency support, or documentation support, but it should not be represented as "required clinical hours" for California CNA renewal unless CDPH/TPRU gives written approval for the exact use case.

**Requires legal/compliance review.** Simulation, virtual simulation, LTI-based scenario tools, or online "clinical" simulations should not be counted toward California CNA renewal clinical hours or in-person/in-service hours unless CDPH provides written approval. No official California CNA renewal source reviewed explicitly authorizes simulation or virtual simulation to replace renewal in-service/CE hours or clinical practice.

## 5. 12-Hour Theory Compliance Analysis

| Question | Finding | Label | Source / Evidence | Compliance Position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Is 12 hours asynchronous theory required by California? | No official source reviewed requires exactly 12 asynchronous theory hours. California requires 48 total renewal hours, 12 minimum each year, and allows up to 24 online. | Not enough evidence found | HSC 1337.6; CDPH 283A/283C. [S1] [S3] [S4] | Do not describe the 12-hour Moodle theory block as a state-required structure. |
| Is asynchronous online theory acceptable as CE? | Potentially yes, but only through a CDPH-approved online computer training program and approved courses, within the 24-hour online cap. | Verified requirement with conditions | HSC 1337.6; CDPH Online CE Providers; CDPH 192B. [S1] [S2] [S6] | Acceptable only with CDPH online CE approval and controls. |
| Is it acceptable as in-service education? | For online CE, yes only as CDPH-approved online CE. Facility in-service has separate rules and may not treat ordinary LMS training as CDPH CE unless approved. | Needs state verification | CDPH 192 states facility LMS courses are not CE credit unless LMS/courses are Department-approved online CE. [S7] | Avoid calling routine employer LMS modules CDPH in-service/CE unless approved. |
| Are 12 online hours enough for renewal? | No. It is partial credit only. California requires 48 hours, and only up to 24 online hours may count. | Verified requirement | HSC 1337.6; CDPH 283A/283C. [S1] [S3] [S4] | Certificate and marketing must state "12 CE hours" and not "complete renewal." |
| Are seat time and logs enough? | No. CDPH requires time/pause controls, but also identity confirmation, interactivity/feedback, exam/test, signed statement, and certificate controls. | Verified requirement | HSC 1337.6; CDPH 192B. [S1] [S6] | Moodle must use a multi-evidence completion model. |
| Is a final exam required? | Yes for online CE courses under CDPH 192B. | Verified requirement | CDPH 192B. [S6] | Moodle quiz/test required before certificate release. |

**Compliance conclusion.** A 12-hour asynchronous Moodle theory component is viable as a California CNA online CE component only if CI Institute of Nursing is approved by CDPH as an online CE provider, the courses are approved, the courses comply with CDPH 192B/HSC 1337.6 controls, and learners are warned that online credit is capped at 24 hours per renewal cycle.

| Records inaccessible during CDPH review | CDPH 192 says records must be immediately accessible to Department upon request. [S7] | Maintain exportable audit packets. | Admin dashboard and standardized export folder. |
| QR verification exposes private data | Public verification could disclose CNA number or completion details. | Tokenized verification with minimal fields. | Verification page shows certificate valid/invalid and learner initials or masked ID only unless authenticated. |

**Recommended privacy disclaimer for learner upload areas.** "Do not upload patient names, faces, medical record numbers, dates of birth, addresses, facility chart screenshots, medication records, or any information that could identify a patient/resident. Upload only de-identified training evidence requested by the course. Submissions containing patient/resident identifiers may be rejected and deleted according to privacy procedures."
