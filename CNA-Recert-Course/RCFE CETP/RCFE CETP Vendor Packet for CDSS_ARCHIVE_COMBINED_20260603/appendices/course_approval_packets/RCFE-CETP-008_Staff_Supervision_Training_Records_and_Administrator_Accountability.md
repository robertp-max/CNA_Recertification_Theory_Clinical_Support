### Course Description

This course focuses on administrator responsibilities for staff supervision, role clarity, onboarding, training records, day/night coverage communication, performance follow-up, and documentation of corrective coaching.

### Measurable Learning Objectives

- Identify staff supervision controls that support resident care and regulatory readiness.
- Explain how training records, duty assignments, and communication tools support accountability.
- Apply a coaching and documentation approach to staff performance scenarios.

### Post-Test Questions and Answer Key

| No. | Question | Answer key |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | What should staff supervision documentation show? | Expectations, observations, coaching, follow-up, and escalation when needed. |
| 2 | Why are training records part of supervision? | They show staff were prepared for assigned duties and support compliance review. |
| 3 | What is a risk of unclear role assignments? | Gaps in care, inconsistent documentation, and reduced accountability. |
| 4 | When should a staffing issue be escalated? | When resident safety, regulatory compliance, or coverage expectations may be affected. |
| 5 | Which Core category applies? | Management/Supervision of Staff. |
