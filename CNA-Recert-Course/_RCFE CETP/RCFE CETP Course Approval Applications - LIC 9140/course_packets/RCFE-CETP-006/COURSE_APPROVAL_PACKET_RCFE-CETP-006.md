### Course Description

This course prepares administrators to manage admission and retention decisions, pre-admission appraisal, physician documentation, reappraisal, needs and services plans, and escalation when resident needs change.

### Measurable Learning Objectives

- Identify records and decision points used in RCFE admission and retention review.
- Explain how appraisal, physician reports, and service planning guide resident care decisions.
- Recognize when a change in condition requires reappraisal, external coordination, or leadership review.
- Apply documentation controls to sample admission and retention scenarios.

### Post-Test Questions and Answer Key

| No. | Question | Answer key |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | What is the function of a needs and services plan? | It translates assessed resident needs into services, monitoring, documentation, and staff direction. |
| 2 | When should reappraisal be considered? | When resident condition, behavior, care needs, or safety risks materially change. |
| 3 | Why should admission decisions be documented carefully? | They support resident safety, regulatory compliance, and defensible operations. |
| 4 | What should happen when retention is uncertain? | Escalate to leadership and Compliance before a final decision. |
